Prem Mandir- The Temple of Divine Love


Prem Mandir (The Temple of Divine Love) is a Hindu temple in Shri Vrindavan Dham, Uttar Pradesh, India, which was established and inaugurated by Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj in February 2012. This temple has become the modern epicentre of Vrindavan, in the holy land of Braj, where Shri Radha Krishna descended about 5,000 years ago and performed divine leelas (pastimes)


Hundreds of thousands of people from all over the world visit this architectural marvel every month. The breathtaking sight of Shri Radha Krishna and Shri Sita Ram evoke the feelings of pure devotion and bliss in the hearts of the devotees. Surrounded by beautiful gardens and fountains, the 55-acre temple premises has life-sized depictions of four leelas of Shri Krishna – Jhulan leela, Govardhan leela, Raas leela and Kaliya Naag leela, which naturally fill the hearts of seekers with devotion.


Why the name – PREM Mandir?

प्रेमाधीन ब्रह्म श्याम वेद ने बताया ।
याते याय नाम ‘प्रेम मन्दिर’ धराया ।।

The Vedas declare the supremacy of divine love.

It is so powerful that even the Supreme Lord Shri

Krishna remains under its control.

This inspired me to give the name ‘Prem Mandir’

to this majestic temple.


Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj
